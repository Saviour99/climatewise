Who We Are

ClimateWISE Youth Organization (CYO) is a dynamic non-profit, youth-led organization committed to advancing climate resilience, water, sanitation, and hygiene (WASH), and sustainable development in communities. We focus on empowering youth, women, and vulnerable groups through education, capacity building, and skills development.

Our Vision

We envision a future where every action, big or small, contributes to the well-being of people, communities, and the environment.

Our Mission

Our mission is to mobilize and equip youth and vulnerable groups to lead climate and WASH solutions that protect ecosystems, confront environmental injustices, and strengthen community resilience.

We do this through education, community-based action, research, and partnerships that strengthen livelihoods, protect ecosystems, and build healthier, more resilient communities.

Thematic Areas

Each thematic area represents a strategic pillar of intervention designed to address critical and interconnected dimensions of community development. Together, these thematic areas form a holistic framework that integrates environmental sustainability, public health, youth leadership, and evidence-based decision-making. The approach recognizes that climate resilience, WASH, environmental management, and youth empowerment are mutually reinforcing and must be addressed simultaneously to achieve lasting and equitable impact at the community level.

ClimateWISE Youth Organization (CYO) prioritizes strengthening community resilience to climate variability and extreme climate events, including floods, prolonged droughts, heat stress, and erratic rainfall patterns. Many vulnerable communities face increasing exposure to climate risks without adequate adaptive capacity, leading to food insecurity, livelihood loss, displacement, and health challenges. CYO addresses these challenges through integrated climate adaptation strategies that combine local knowledge, scientific evidence, and youth leadership.

Key interventions include climate-smart agriculture practices that enhance food security while conserving soil and water resources, ecosystem restoration initiatives such as tree planting, wetland protection, and watershed rehabilitation, and community-based disaster risk reduction programs. CYO supports early warning systems, preparedness planning, and post-disaster recovery actions to reduce loss of life and livelihoods. Youth-led adaptation projects are central to this thematic area, ensuring that young people actively design and implement solutions within their communities.

Special emphasis is placed on the inclusion of women, persons with disabilities, and other vulnerable groups who are often disproportionately affected by climate impacts. The organization also promotes renewable energy adoption, energy efficiency, and low-carbon technologies to reduce emissions while expanding access to sustainable energy services. Through research, community engagement, and capacity building, CYO enables communities to transition from reactive responses to proactive, locally driven climate action.

Thematic Areas

Each thematic area represents a strategic pillar of intervention designed to address critical and interconnected dimensions of community development. Together, these thematic areas form a holistic framework that integrates environmental sustainability, public health, youth leadership, and evidence-based decision-making. The approach recognizes that climate resilience, WASH, environmental management, and youth empowerment are mutually reinforcing and must be addressed simultaneously to achieve lasting and equitable impact at the community level.

CYO recognizes access to safe water, adequate sanitation, and good hygiene practices as fundamental human rights and essential pillars of public health, dignity, and socioeconomic development. In many communities, inadequate WASH services contribute to waterborne diseases, poor educational outcomes, reduced productivity, and increased vulnerability to climate-induced water stress.

Under this thematic area, CYO supports the development and rehabilitation of community water supply systems, including boreholes, small-scale treatment systems, rainwater harvesting, and climate-resilient water infrastructure. Sanitation interventions focus on promoting improved household and communal sanitation facilities, ending open defecation, and supporting locally appropriate sanitation technologies.

Equally important is behavior change communication. CYO implements hygiene education campaigns targeting handwashing, safe water handling, menstrual hygiene management, and environmental sanitation. Youth are trained as WASH ambassadors to serve as peer educators and community mobilizers, ensuring sustainability beyond project lifespans. By integrating infrastructure development, education, and strong community participation, CYO aims to reduce disease burdens, improve resilience to water scarcity, and strengthen local systems for long-term WASH management.

CYO advances environmental sustainability by promoting responsible production and consumption patterns and addressing the growing challenge of waste pollution. Poor waste management practices contribute to environmental degradation, flooding, public health risks, and greenhouse gas emissions. CYO adopts a circular economy approach that views waste as a resource and an opportunity for local economic development.

The organization implements community-based waste reduction, segregation, recycling, and upcycling initiatives, with a strong emphasis on youth participation and entrepreneurship. Programs support eco-innovation, skills training for waste collectors and recyclers, and the development of small green enterprises that convert plastic, organic waste, and other materials into marketable products.

Behavioral change campaigns encourage households and businesses to reduce waste generation and adopt sustainable consumption habits. CYO also works in partnership with local authorities, private sector actors, and informal waste workers to strengthen waste management systems and policy implementation. Through these combined efforts, CYO contributes to cleaner environments, reduced pollution, job creation, and the development of regenerative local economies that balance environmental protection with social and economic benefits.

Youth empowerment is a cross-cutting priority and a core driver of CYO’s mission. Young people represent a significant proportion of the population and hold immense potential to lead transformative change when equipped with the right knowledge, skills, and opportunities. CYO invests in building the technical, leadership, and entrepreneurial capacities of youth to enable them to actively contribute to sustainable development and climate resilience.

Programs under this thematic area include climate literacy workshops, WASH technical trainings, environmental leadership bootcamps, mentorship schemes, and innovation challenges. These initiatives combine theory with hands-on learning, problem-solving, and community engagement. CYO fosters youth networks and platforms that enable peer learning, collaboration, and leadership development at the local and national levels.

By nurturing confident, skilled, and socially responsible young leaders, CYO strengthens community ownership of development initiatives and ensures the continuity and scalability of climate and environmental interventions over time.
